Free boundary minimal surfaces with arbitrary topology

Conjecture (Realisation problem).
Surfaces of arbitrary topological type can be realised as embedded free boundary minimal surfaces with area strictly below 2π in the three-dimensional Euclidean unit ball.

Given any pair of natural numbers g and b, we can arrange g necks and b half-necks arbitrarily at the corners of a (g+n)-gon in order to glue two parallel discs. The resulting surface has genus g and b boundary components (unless b=0, in which case, the surface has genus g-1 and 2 boundary components). The following simulations indicate that any such configuration can be deformed into a free boundary minimal surface in the unit ball. The respective symmetry group may vary, but each surface in this family has at least one plane of reflection.
